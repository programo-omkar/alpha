Mahabaleshwar

Mahabaleshwar is a small town and a municipal council in Satara district, Maharashtra, India. It is a place of pilgrimage for Hindus because the Krishna river has its origin here. The British colonial rulers developed the town as a hill station, and served as the summer capital of Bombay Presidency during the British Raj.

Mahabaleshwar: A popular hill station in the Western Ghats, known for its natural beauty, scenic views, and strawberry farms. It is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Mahabaleshwar is famous for its strawberries and farm fresh strawberries and raspberries are available throughout the year. You can also shop for handicrafts, leather items, Kolhapuri footwear, and more from the Town Bazaar. The town is also known for its numerous rivers, magnificent cascades, and majestic peaks, making it a popular weekend getaway from Pune and Mumbai.

The town has an elevation of 1,353 meters (4,439 ft) above sea level and is situated in the Western Ghats. It is about 120 km southwest of Pune and 285 km from Mumbai. The town has a population of 12,737 people as of 2011, with a sex ratio of 90 females per 100 males and a literacy rate of 78%.
